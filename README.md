This research report is an investigation into applying Zero Trust Architectural patterns to Cyber Physical systems in both cloud based distributed systems and centralized locations. The paper explores essentials of Zero Trust Architecture over perimeter based models, components of Cyber-Physical systems such as SCADA and sensors, Identity and Access Management, micro-segmentation, next generation firewalls, cloud-based cyber-physical systems, machine learning implications within CPS, and case studies. Relevant literature review for considerations are included. This report was a part of CYBV 660-Zero Trust Architecture, as part of the Masters of Science in Cyber and Information Operations degree program at the University of Arizona College of Information Science, an NSA designated CAE-CO (Cyber Operations), Cyber Defense (CAE-CD), and Research (CAE-R) university.
